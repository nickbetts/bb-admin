Parent Questions

Everything Parents Ask Us

Can my child travel to the UK on their own? +
Yes — many of our players travel unaccompanied from countries all around the world. We offer airport meet-and-greet transfers and have 24/7 welfare staff on-site throughout. Parents receive daily updates from the moment their child lands.
How will I be kept updated while they’re at camp? +
You’ll receive daily WhatsApp updates including photos and short video clips of training. A parent helpline is available 24/7, and your child has access to Wi-Fi throughout camp so they can call and message freely.
How are dietary requirements and allergies managed? +
The catering team is fully briefed on every player’s dietary needs before camp begins. We accommodate vegetarian, vegan, halal, kosher, coeliac, and all allergen requirements. Please provide details in the booking form.
What is the refund policy if plans change? +
The £200 deposit is fully refundable within 14 days of booking. After that, the deposit is non-refundable but the balance is refundable up to 8 weeks before camp. We advise taking out comprehensive travel insurance to cover cancellation.
Does my child need a visa to attend? +
Players from outside the UK may require a Standard Visitor Visa. As this is a sports programme, most nationalities can apply for a 6-month visitor visa. We provide a full, official invitation letter to support the application at no extra cost — typically issued within 48 hours of booking.
What happens if my child gets injured or unwell? +
We have first-aid trained staff on duty at all sessions and on-site at all times. Any incident is logged and parents are notified immediately. For more serious matters, we work with local medical facilities and will contact you directly. We strongly recommend travel insurance.
What level does my child need to be at? +
Players of all levels above recreational are welcome — from organised club football through to academy-level players. Coaches assess each player on day one and group sessions are structured accordingly. Every player is challenged and developed at their own level.
What should my child pack? +
Football boots (firm and soft ground), shin pads, casual wear, trainers, and personal toiletries. Training kits are provided. A full, detailed packing list is sent in the welcome pack after booking. We recommend packing light — there’s no need for formal wear at any point.
Is travel insurance compulsory? +
It is not compulsory but we strongly recommend it. A comprehensive travel insurance policy covering medical treatment, cancellation, and personal belongings is advisable for all players, particularly those travelling from overseas.
Can I visit my child during camp? +
Camp is a residential programme and the full experience is designed around players immersing themselves in the environment. We do not facilitate mid-camp visits but parents are always welcome to contact the welfare team and receive updates throughout.
