The Football

What You'll Actually Learn

Sessions are run by UEFA-qualified coaches and former professionals. Two weeks of progressive, structured football that makes a real difference.

🥊

Beat Your Defender

1v1 attacking play, quick feet, close control, and the confidence to take players on. Drills designed to make the skills stick in match situations.

🧠

Read the Game

Positioning, pressing patterns, tactical shape, and understanding what happens before you get the ball. The part most coaches skip.

🏋

Finishing & Shooting

Dedicated finishing drills, angles, placement, first-time shots. Whether you're a striker or coming forward from midfield, goals get scored here.

🕵

Goalkeeping Specialism

GK sessions run by dedicated specialist coaches covering shot-stopping, distribution, and commanding your area. Not just general training.

🎯

Video Analysis

Professional film analysis sessions where coaches break down your own performance. The same tool used by Premier League academies.

🤺

Strength & Recovery

Age-appropriate conditioning, sports science, and proper recovery sessions so you can perform at your best every single day of the programme.

Player FAQ

Stuff Players Actually Ask

Is there Wi-Fi? Can I use my phone? +
Yes to both. Wi-Fi is available throughout the camp complex. Your phone is yours to use in your own time. Most players video call home in the evenings and share content from training and excursions. During sessions, phones are away.
What if I don't know anyone there? +
Almost no one arrives knowing people. That's the whole point. Players from 40+ countries all in the same place. By day two you'll have made friends you genuinely didn't expect. It's one of the things every single player mentions.
What level do I need to be? +
Club level and above. You need to be playing regular organised football. We welcome club, regional, and academy-level players. Coaches assess every player on day one and sessions are structured to challenge whatever level you're at.
Is there specialist goalkeeping coaching? +
Yes, dedicated GK coaching sessions with specialist coaches are built into the programme. Not just standing in goal during outfield drills. Real, specific goalkeeping work.
What should I pack? +
Football boots (firm and soft ground), shin pads, casual clothes, trainers, and toiletries. Training kits are provided, no need to bring your own. A full packing list is sent with your booking confirmation. Travel light.
Do I have to travel to the UK alone? +
Not at all. Many players travel alone and our team provides meet-and-greet transfers from major UK airports. We also offer unaccompanied minor support for younger players. Your parents or guardians receive full arrival and welfare updates throughout.
What happens in the evenings? +
Evening activities are a huge part of camp. There's a players' hub with Xbox, pool tables, and ping pong on-site. Across the two weeks, organised activities include paintball, escape rooms, adventure courses, and other off-site excursions.
Do I get any feedback on my football? +
Yes. Coaches provide individual feedback throughout the camp, and every player receives a written end-of-camp review noting specific areas of improvement and strengths. Film analysis sessions are included where you watch your own performance back.
What level of English do I need? +
Conversational English is helpful but not required to participate in the football. Football is a universal language. If you want to improve your English during camp, optional language classes are available for £200 per week.
Can I come back the following year? +
Absolutely, and many players do. Returning players often find a completely different experience as they progress in their development. There are no restrictions on attending multiple camps across different years.
